Our Awesome Chef's

1.Chef-Owner :-

National average salary: ₹2,99,732 per year Primary duties: A chef-owner begins and owns their own restaurant. They often focus on business management tasks, such as establishing the restaurant and overseeing its operations. While chef-owners may continue to work in kitchens, their primary involvement with cooking pertains to creating and updating the menu. Also known as a group chef, their role combines culinary expertise with business acumen. As the owner of a restaurant or culinary business, the chef-owner is responsible for overseeing all aspects of the operation, including menu development, staff hiring, and financial planning. As Head Chef you will oversee the day-to-day running of the kitchens and be able to manage, lead, motivate and develop the team. Responsibilities will include menu planning, managing the financial KPI's enabling business success, purchasing, health and safety including food hygiene procedures.

2.Head-Chef :-

National average salary: ₹ 4,47,833 per year Primary duties: A head chef may be the same as an executive chef but this depends on the specific restaurant and its kitchen hierarchy. They monitor kitchen activities, maintain an inventory, order supplies and sometimes train new employees. They may also maintain relationships with vendors, oversee the kitchen and its employees and communicate with other staff members and customers. As the head chef, your responsibilities will include creating menus, ordering ingredients and supplies, managing inventory, overseeing food preparation and cooking, ensuring food safety and quality standards are met, managing the kitchen staff, and ensuring that the kitchen operates efficiently and effectively. Chefs and head cooks oversee the daily food preparation at restaurants and other places where food is served. They direct kitchen staff and handle any food-related concerns.

3.Pantry-Chef :-

National average salary: ₹ 1,50,000 per year Primary duties: A pantry chef manages refrigerator stocks and prepares cold dishes. They typically work in larger restaurants that have several cold storage areas that need a dedicated person to monitor supply levels and ensure all food is fresh. Pantry chefs could also prepare salads and other cold meals, order new supplies and deliver cold items to chefs who need them. A pantry chef, also known as a garde manger, is a kitchen, professional who prepares cold dishes, sometimes hot meals, and manages the cold food storage area. A garde manger, also called a pantry chef, is the specialty chef in charge of cold food items and storage. They prepare and maintain cold foods such as salads, fruits, cheeses and preserved meats. Some restaurants also call the cold food storage area "garde manger."

4.Executive-Chef :-

National average salary: ₹ 4,58,809 per year Primary duties: An executive chef is the highest-ranking member of the kitchen and it requires several years of education and experience to advance to this position. They manage all kitchen activities and oversee each chef and cook to ensure they complete their tasks efficiently. Executive chefs may not cook during normal operating hours but they often help create menus, review final plate presentations and taste dishes before the waitstaff takes them to customers. An executive chef, also known as a head chef or master chef, is responsible for managing all aspects of a restaurant's kitchen and culinary operations. The main differences between an executive chef and a head chef lie in their duties. Executive chefs typically have more managerial responsibilities, while head chefs work more closely with the kitchen team and may do some cooking.
